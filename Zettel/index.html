Zettelkasten Note or Zettel is a minimal semantic unit, info-atom and the basic level of the KnowledgeBase. The basic rule is

one note â€“ one thought

Notes should be written in an academic (scientific) style: so that you can use1 them in the Content Boxes as it is.

During The Experiment, I decided to keep a length note limit equal to the tweet size2:

280 characters without links

The character limit allows being laser-focused on one thought and keeps Content Boxes straightforward and minimal. But enough to find all the information and links3 you need to dig further.

Following the 5Ws, one of the values2 is written to note tags in YAML format:

[opening, who, what, when, where, why, how, closing]

Unlike the Ever Green approach, Zettels, in in_progress/Knowledge Forging Methodology, are crystallized, mature ideas that rarely change. This is a fundamental difference between PKB and CKB.

Personal Knowledge Base (PKB) is a collection of your and others’ thoughts for further reflection and getting insights and ideas. In other words, bias toward creativity (creator mode)4. The goals of corporate KB (CKB) are to preserve and transfer already formed, existing knowledge (archivist mode)5.
